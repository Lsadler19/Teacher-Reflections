5 May 2026Year: 9 | e1

Focus: Cricket - Close Catching

What? - The lesson had an in depth plan and the lesson followed the plan well. However as the class was very mixed ability, the catching task was far too easy for some pupils and too difficult for others. This lead to pupils becoming disruptive. Testing my behaviour management strategies. So what? - This lead to low level chatter and constant disruption from a few pupils making everything take twice as long as I had to keep reminding them of expectations. Now what? - This lesson has made me aware that I need to focus on the pitch of the lesson and due to the classes being mixed ability, have a clear and planned way of how to scaffold or stretch and challenge every task completed. Also this had made it clear to me that I need to become more firm with the behaviour policy to show pupils that my expectations in the classroom are not flexible. Short Term target - Send a pupil to reflection before the end of the half term. Once I have done this once I will feel more confident doing it and following the behaviour policy to resolution.
5 May 2026Year: 9 | e3

Focus: 1600m - Pacing Strategies

What? - This felt like a strong lesson to start the week. The lesson plan had thought about how long each section of the lesson would take and what the discussion would include. Planning hinge questions and anticipating misconceptions within the plan allowed for fluent responsive teaching. So what? - The depth of the lesson plan allowed me to always feel confident in delivery. I knew exactly what I was going to talk about in the discussions and what misconceptions I was going to tackle. This lead to whole class understanding of the topic which was confirmed through pre planned hinge questions. Now what? - I will continue to plan with this much depth to ensure a fluent lesson that focuses on addressing misconceptions and ensuring whole class understanding. I felt that what made this lesson stand out was the focus that went into the discussion , before the lesson I planned all the talking points I was going to cover and the questions I was going to ask. To develop my teaching practice further - I will aim to make this consistent.
